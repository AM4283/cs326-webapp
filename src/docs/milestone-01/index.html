Overview

In the age of the rapidly growing internet, online shopping has become a convenience billions of people rely on worldwide. Especially in a post-Covid world, where every aspect of our lives has become more solitary, and we are more conscious now than ever about leaving our homes, buying goods online, including clothing, has become an affordable, safe, and extremely popular option. What we aim to solve with this project is navigating the vast internet to find the best products at the best prices. StyleScout 360 is a web app designed to help consumers get the best price on the items they're looking for in one seamless experience, without having to visit every site individually and manually compare prices. StyleScout finds products the consumer wants on a variety of online retailers and deliveries them with a link to purchase them, saving the customer the work of having to find the best price themself, only to realize later that they missed a site with a better price. StyleScout is something anyone who shops online can benefit from making use of.

Application Parts

The most important component of the StyleScout is, of course, the search feature. The search feature is what allows the consumer to find the product that they're looking for. There are many different ways to search on StyleScout, the simplest being to type in key words about the product, which will return a variety of results. Users can also search for a specific product and narrow down their results using filters, until they find a product they're satisfied with. From there, the next component comes in, which is the real-time list of prices of any given product on a range of popular online shopping sites. This will allow the user to compare prices, shipping costs, and other store-specific information about the product they're interested in. Finally, the pinnacle of convenience, is the wish list. While browsing for items, users can add items they find interesting to their personal wish list, which they can access at a later date if they're not ready to commit to buying it. They can also ask to be notified via email to remind them about the product, or get updates on new lower prices or a new seller becoming available. Combined with the ease of comparing prices across retailers, this component gives the user the most convenient way to shop online. StyleScout gives the user all their favorite brands in one place, without the hassle of finding deals themself.

Wire Frames

The wireframes below showcase the main functions of StyleScout360, as they represent the pages that users will consistently utilize. Each of these different wireframes of StyleScout360 will first and foremost include a navbar that houses the site logo in the top left which acts as a “home” button, a search bar to search for products, a button to look in one’s current cart, and an account button to access account settings or log-in if the user is not signed-in.

The “Home page” is the main view of StyleScout360 that users will see when they first enter the site. It is designed to welcome the user & immerse the user in various products that are being indexed. As a logged-in user, they will be able to view their current orders as well as relative tracking info that can be clicked to see more in-depth information. Additionally, they will be able to see their recently viewed items in case they return to continue their shopping. On the sidebar, we have: An orders tab that would take the user to the additional details page of their recent orders, A wishlist tab that would take the user to their saved items that they may have wanted to buy later (or ask someone else to!) A settings tab that also lists some basic sections like privacy and security where users could go to modify their login information, whether or not their search history is being tracked, etc. A saved info panel that lets users quickly access their saved payments, search history, and addresses so that they can modify them if they wished If a user is not signed in, the home page would instead include a recommendation view that indexes the current most popular items based on site metrics, say previous visits that the user may have had on the site.

The search page is what a user would see after looking up their keywords to try and find relevant products. On this page, like many other shopping sites, they will be presented with a list of results of products that match their keywords. They have the option to filter these results by the site (say they want the shirt to be for sure available at a specific retailer), by size, color, etc.

On the product details page that a user would encounter after selecting a product they wish to view, we find the main appeal of StyleScout 360: our price comparison from multiple retailers. On this page, we see the product and associated images, as well as a list of retailers that are currently selling the product as well as the price that they are selling it at, as well as the number of them they have available for say, the selected size, if applicable. On this page, the user can sort the different store providers by price, stock, ratings, etc., and can add the item to their cart (with a quantity count if they want to add multiple) or potentially to their wishlist. If the user scrolls down, they can find a summarized details section that tries to display things like the material & measurements of the piece of clothing, if known.

The checkout/cart page shows what the user would see after they have clicked on their cart and are ready to either check out or remove items from the cart. On this page, they see the list of items in their cart, the stores they are trying to buy them from, the quantity, and the item total (before shipping & tax). The user then has the option to add store-applicable coupon codes, as well as checkout at the bottom of the page. On our sidebar, we have quick access to our saved addresses and payment methods if those need changing (for logged-in users).

Integrative Experience

The StyleScout 360 project ties in deeply with the foundations of the Integrative Experience requirement at UMass as the various components of the app require skill to execute that is the culmination of what one has learned during their time as not only a CS undergrad but an undergrad on the diverse campus at UMass. The problem that StyleScout 360 attempts to solve is very applicable to the real world, and at its core, a humanitarian one. With the advent of the digital era and online shopping being more prevalent than ever, people are increasingly relying on digital storefronts to buy the styles they need. Unlike many other products, shopping for clothes requires one to jump through many different sites to compare prices, deals, etc., leading many consumers to waste much of their valuable time scouring the web to cop a brand-new style. Understanding this problem and how one could design a solution such as ours is a core part of the general education curriculum presented at UMass. The solution is a collaborative endeavor and must be presented in an intuitive and easy-to-understand application, all of which is the goal of the general education curriculum.

In terms of the technical aspects of the solution, the creation of the StyleScout 360 is no easy task. Much of the app requires at least an intermediate understanding of various functional programming, data structures, and algorithms concepts to design a full-stack web app that attempts to interface with so many other APIs across the web. If it were to be deployed in the real world, the application must be responsive, intuitive, and fast. The search algorithms being used must be efficient, the handlers for user events robust, and the data structures used to parse API calls to other clothing sites understandable. These are all core skills that are taught in the CS undergraduate curriculum at UMass that can be interconnected with each other to solve the online clothing shopping predicament.
